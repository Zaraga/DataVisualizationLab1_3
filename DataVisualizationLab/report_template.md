# Data Visualization Lab Writeup

## Distribution summary
I visualized the distributions of BPM, key, mode, danceability, energy, speechiness, and acousticness.

From the dataset, the most common and typical values were:
- BPM: 100 to 140
- Key: C#
- Mode: Major
- Danceability: 57 to 78
- Energy: 53 to 77
- Speechiness: 4 to 11
- Acousticness: 6 to 43

## Guaranteed smash hit profile
Based on the visualizations and calculations, a song that matches the most common profile would:
- be in C#
- use Major mode
- have a BPM between 100 and 140
- have danceability between 57 and 78
- have energy between 53 and 77
- have speechiness between 4 and 11
- have acousticness between 6 and 43

## Songs in the dataset that match
The songs in the dataset that matched all of those conditions were:
- Need To Know - Doja Cat
- Ginseng Strip 2002 - Yung Lean

Of these, Need To Know appears to have been more popular based on its streams and chart presence.

## Compare a known smash hit
I compared Blinding Lights by The Weeknd.

It matches the common key and mode, but several other values fall outside the typical ranges:
- BPM is much higher
- danceability is slightly lower
- energy is slightly higher
- acousticness is much lower

This shows that hit songs can share some common traits, but they do not have to match every common feature exactly.
